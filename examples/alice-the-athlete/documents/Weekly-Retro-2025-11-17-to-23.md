## Experiments to Run (Week 5)

### Experiment 1: Sleep Prioritization Protocol üò¥

**Hypothesis:** Consistent 7-8 hours sleep on nights before training days will improve workout performance and recovery.

**Implementation:**
- Sunday, Tuesday, Thursday nights = strict 22:00 bedtime (training Monday, Wednesday, Friday)
- "Devices down" at 21:30 on these nights
- Morning alarm 06:00 = 8 hours sleep window

**Success metric:**
- All Week 5 training runs completed with HR staying in Z2
- Post-workout energy levels rated 7/10 or higher
- Next-day soreness rated 3/5 or lower

**If this works:** Make it permanent protocol for all training weeks

### Experiment 2: Daily Hydration Target üíß

**Hypothesis:** Consistent hydration (64oz daily) will improve run performance and recovery markers.

**Implementation:**
- Morning routine: 16oz water upon waking (before coffee)
- Work hours: 32oz (refill 16oz bottle twice)
- Evening: 16oz minimum
- Track: Urine color check (aim for pale yellow)

**Success metric:**
- Morning urine consistently pale yellow
- No mid-run dehydration symptoms (dry mouth, dizziness)
- Post-run recovery feels smoother

**Data to collect:** Urine color morning check (simple visual)

### Experiment 3: Standardized Pre-Run Fueling ‚è∞

**Hypothesis:** 1 banana + 16oz water at exactly 90 minutes before run = optimal energy without GI issues.

**Implementation:**
- Set timer/alarm for 90min before planned run start
- Consume: 1 medium banana + 16oz water
- No other food closer than 90min to run

**Success metric:**
- No GI discomfort during any Week 5 runs
- Energy levels feel consistent through all run intervals
- No "bonking" feeling in latter portions

**Iteration:** If 90min doesn't work, try 75min or 105min
